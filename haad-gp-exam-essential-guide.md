Abu Dhabi, the capital of the United Arab Emirates, offers exceptional career opportunities for General Practitioners with competitive salaries, world-class healthcare facilities, and a high standard of living. To practice medicine in Abu Dhabi, you must pass the **HAAD-GP licensing exam** (now administered by the Department of Health - DOH).

This guide provides essential information about the HAAD-GP exam to help you prepare efficiently and pass on your first attempt.

## Important Note: HAAD to DOH Transition

In 2019, the Health Authority Abu Dhabi (HAAD) merged with other regulatory bodies to form the **Department of Health (DOH) Abu Dhabi**. While many professionals still refer to it as "HAAD exam," the official licensing authority is now DOH.

**Key Points:**
- The exam format and requirements remain similar
- DOH now handles all medical licensing for Abu Dhabi emirate
- The exam is still commonly called "HAAD exam" in medical circles
- Visit our [HAAD exam page](/exams/haad) for the latest updates

## HAAD-GP Exam Format

Understanding the exam structure is crucial for effective preparation:

**Exam Specifications:**
- **Total Questions:** 150 multiple choice questions (MCQs)
- **Duration:** 165 minutes (2 hours 45 minutes)
- **Passing Score:** 60% (90 correct answers out of 150)
- **Question Format:** Single best answer with clinical scenarios
- **Delivery:** Computer-based test at Prometric centers
- **Language:** English
- **Negative Marking:** No penalties for incorrect answers

**Time Management:** You have approximately 66 seconds per question. Efficient time management is essential for completing all questions.

## HAAD-GP Syllabus Overview

The HAAD exam covers comprehensive medical knowledge across major specialties with emphasis on general practice competencies:

### Subject Distribution

**Internal Medicine (30-35%)**
- Cardiovascular diseases, respiratory conditions, gastroenterology
- Endocrine disorders (diabetes, thyroid), nephrology, hematology
- Infectious diseases relevant to UAE clinical practice

**Pediatrics (15-20%)**
- Growth and development, immunizations (UAE schedule)
- Common childhood infections, respiratory conditions
- Pediatric emergencies and acute care

**Obstetrics & Gynecology (15-20%)**
- Antenatal and postnatal care, labor management
- Gynecological conditions, contraception, menopause
- High-risk pregnancy identification

**Surgery (10-15%)**
- Acute abdomen, common surgical conditions
- Trauma management, pre and post-operative care

**Emergency Medicine (8-10%)**
- Cardiovascular emergencies, acute respiratory distress
- Trauma protocols, toxicology basics

**Psychiatry (5-8%)**
- Depression, anxiety disorders, psychotic conditions
- Substance abuse, emergency psychiatry

**Other Specialties (10-15%)**
- Dermatology, Ophthalmology, ENT
- Preventive medicine, public health
- Medical ethics and professionalism

### High-Yield Topics

Focus your preparation on these commonly tested areas:

✓ Diabetes mellitus management and complications

✓ Asthma and COPD management
✓ Pediatric vaccination schedules
✓ Prenatal screening and high-risk pregnancy

## Eligibility and Application Process

### Basic Requirements

**Educational Qualifications:**
- MBBS/MD degree from recognized university
- 12 months rotating internship completion
- Minimum 2 years post-internship GP experience
- Valid medical license from home country

**Documentation:**
- All educational certificates (attested)
- Experience certificates from employers
- Good Standing Certificate (issued within 3 months)
- DataFlow Primary Source Verification
- Valid passport and photographs

### Application Steps

**1. DataFlow Verification (8-12 weeks)**
- Register at dataflowgroup.com
- Submit all documents for verification
- Cost: Approximately $250-350

**2. DOH Application (2-4 weeks)**
- Create account on DOH licensing portal
- Upload attested documents and DataFlow report
- Pay application fee: AED 1,000 ($272)

**3. Exam Registration**
- Receive eligibility letter from DOH
- Schedule exam through Prometric
- Pay exam fee: AED 1,000 ($272)

**4. Take Exam**
- Appear at Prometric center
- Complete 150 MCQs in 165 minutes
- Receive immediate preliminary result

## Exam Day Strategy

**Before the Exam:**
✓ Arrive 30 minutes early at Prometric center
✓ Bring valid passport and confirmation documents
✓ Get adequate sleep (7-8 hours)
✓ Eat a nutritious meal

**During the Exam:**

**First Pass (60-70 minutes):**
- Answer all questions you know confidently
- Don't spend more than 60 seconds on any question
- Mark uncertain questions for review

**Second Pass (60-70 minutes):**
- Return to marked questions
- Use elimination technique
- Make educated guesses

**Final Review (30-35 minutes):**
- Verify all questions answered
- Double-check flagged questions
- Don't change answers unless certain

**Pro Tip:** Trust your first instinct. Studies show first answers are correct 75% of the time when you change them out of anxiety.

## After Passing: Getting Licensed

**License Application:**
- Secure employment from Abu Dhabi healthcare facility
- Employer sponsors your professional license
- Submit final documents to DOH
- Pay license fee: AED 500-1,000

**License Validity:** 2 years (renewable)

**Annual CME Requirement:** Minimum 20 CME credits required for renewal
